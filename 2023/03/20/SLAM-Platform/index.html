Category: Categories: SLAM

Publication:

Junren Sun, Fanchen Meng, Wanyue Li, Xin Meng, Bocheng Zhu*,”A Robust, Real-time, Tightly Coupled GNSS-Visual-Inertial Navigation System”,IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems (T-ITS)(under review)

Description:

I collaborated with Peking University students to develop a SLAM platform. Our project aimed to address the challenge of GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite System) positioning accuracy degradation or complete loss caused by satellite signal blockage and multipath effects when navigating urban high-rise areas or underground parking lots. To overcome this, we devised a positioning solution that incorporates GNSS technology alongside fisheye and binocular cameras.

Responsibilities:

My primary role in this project was centered on the hardware aspect. I was responsible for writing ROS (Robot Operating System) drivers for various sensors, including fisheye cameras, depth cameras, GNSS, radar, and lidar. Moreover, I designed a circuit board capable of synchronous triggering for all sensors.
